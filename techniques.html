sequencing extinct genomes

Paleogenomics comprises a set of techniques for sequencing ancient genomes through the careful extraction of preserved DNA samples. In contrast to modern genome sequencing from live samples, paleogenomic research must take abundant precautions in order to avoid damaging samples. The risk of losing a sample is far greater, as there is only a finite (and usually miniscule) amount of DNA that is preserved well enough to be studied. Samples usually come from fossils or remains that have been well-preserved by their environment.
