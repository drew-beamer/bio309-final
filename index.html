de-extinct

Genomics is enabling scientists to bring back extinct species. But should we?

overview

If you have seen the Jurassic Park films, you might be concerned to hear that scientists want to bring back extinct species. However, the goal of de-extinction work is not to create a theme park for dinosaurs, but rather to bring back biodiversity through studying recently extinct species' genomes. This may not always look like bringing a Tyrannosaurus rex back to lifeâ€”the goal of de-extinction work could be to reintroduce certain lost alleles into populations, increasing biodiversity more incrementally.

Global biodiversity is rapidly declining, with more species going extinct every year and endangered species coming under more and more threat from human activities. One unorthodox area of research resisting this decline is de-extinction - the study of how we can use paleogenomics to identify, sequence, and even restore the genomes of extinct species.
